What is social media campaign?
A social media campaign refers to a coordinated marketing or promotional effort that utilises social media platforms to achieve specific goals. It involves creating and sharing content on social media channels to engage with a target audience, build brand awareness, drive website traffic, generate leads, or promote a product, service, or cause.
Why social media campaign?
Social media campaigns offer several advantages that make them a preferred marketing strategy for many businesses. Here are some reasons why social media campaigns are considered better in certain aspects:
1. Wide Reach: Social media platforms have billions of active users worldwide, providing businesses with the opportunity to reach a vast audience. With targeted advertising options, businesses can reach specific demographics and interests, ensuring their campaigns are seen by the right people.
2. Cost-Effective: Compared to traditional marketing channels like print, television, or radio, social media campaigns are generally more cost-effective. Businesses can allocate budgets based on their needs and have the flexibility to adjust spending as they see fit.
2. Cost-Effective: Compared to traditional marketing channels like print, television, or radio, social media campaigns are generally more cost-effective. Businesses can allocate budgets based on their needs and have the flexibility to adjust spending as they see fit.
3. Enhanced Targeting: Social media platforms provide advanced targeting options, allowing businesses to focus their campaigns on specific demographics, locations, interests, or behaviours.
4. Increased Engagement: Social media campaigns offer interactive features that encourage user engagement. Users can like, share, comment, and tag others, helping to amplify the reach of the campaign.
5. Real-Time Feedback: Social media campaigns provide businesses with the opportunity to receive immediate feedback from their audience. Users can comment, share their opinions, and ask questions, allowing businesses to respond promptly and engage in direct conversations. This real-time feedback helps businesses understand their audience better and make necessary adjustments to their strategies.
6. Measurable Results: Social media platforms offer robust analytics and insights that provide valuable data on campaign performance. Businesses can track metrics such as reach, impressions, engagement, click-through rates, conversions, and more.
7. Viral Potential: Social media campaigns have the potential to go viral and reach a massive audience within a short period. Compelling and shareable content can spread rapidly, generating organic reach and increasing brand visibility exponentially.
Design process
Research
  • Analyse the market landscape and competitor offerings.
  • Identify key insights and opportunities based on research findings.

UX Strategy
  • Create user personas and user journeys to represent target users and their interactions with the product.
  • Define the product's value proposition and core features.

Ideation
  • Generate a range of design ideas and concepts.
  • Sketch or create site map and user flow for the feature.

Wireframe
  • Sketch or create wireframes to visualise the user interface (UI) and interaction flow.
  • Conduct brainstorming sessions and collaborate with the team to refine and iterate on design concepts.

Design
  • Design consistent layouts, typography, colours, icons, and other visual elements.
  • Ensure the UI is intuitive, user-friendly, and visually appealing.
